A portmanteau is the fusion of two words coming together to create a new word. It was first used by Lewis Carroll in 1871, based on the concept of two words packed together, like a portmanteau (“a travelling case having two halves joined by a hinge”). Writers use portmanteaus to make their word choice more interesting.

One thing that many people get Portmanteaus confused with is compound words. Portmanteau are different from compound words.

A compound word is a combination of two or more words that function as a single unit of meaning and the spelling of the two words doesn't change.

For example:
  • Sun + Flower = Sunflower
  • Star + Fish = Starfish
  • Snow + Man = Snowman
  • Snow + Man = Snowman
